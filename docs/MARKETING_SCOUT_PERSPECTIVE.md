**Voice**: Ravi Menon, Chief Scout, Kerala Blasters FC. 15 years in Indian football scouting. Lives in Kochi. Reports to Rajah Rizwan (Academy Director). Skeptical by trade.

This document is written as Ravi would write it — not as marketing would write at Ravi. It is the scout-side argument for why OnlyKrida earns a slot in the Kerala Blasters scouting budget, and the script for converting his peers at Sreenidi Deccan, FC Goa, NEUFC, Punjab FC, and Sporting Club Delhi.

## 1. The scout's reality

### A typical week

Monday morning is WhatsApp. Three hundred unread messages across nine groups — "TFA U-15 trials", "Kerala Premier League coaches", "NEUFC scout notes", "Goa fixtures", "Tamil Nadu youth FA", a private group of seven scouts who've worked together since 2014, and three I started myself for affiliated academies. Half the messages are 38-second clips of kids I'll never meet, shot in vertical, with no context — no age, no position, no team, no opponent. One in fifty is worth opening twice.

Tuesday I'm on a flight or a train. Thiruvananthapuram for the KFA U-17 final. Coimbatore for an FC Madras-hosted regional meet. Kochi for the Kerala Premier League. I land, drop my bag, head to the ground, watch 90 minutes, talk to two coaches whose word I trust, write notes in a Google Sheet on my phone. I shoot my own clips on a Sony alpha 6400 and dump them onto a 1TB Seagate at home. I label folders by date and venue because I'm not disciplined enough to label by player.

Wednesday and Thursday I make calls. Coaches I trained with at NIS Patiala. The grassroots officer at Forca Goa Foundation. A father in Malappuram whose son I tracked for two seasons. A sports journalist at Khel Now who covers I-League. I am triangulating one player against three sources before I put him on Rajah's desk.

Friday is the hardest day. I write the shortlist for U-19 trials. Twenty names. Each one is a bet — a flight ticket, two days of staff time, a ground rental. Wrong names are visible. Right names are invisible until two seasons later.

### The toolkit

- **WhatsApp** — primary inbox, primary outbox, primary archive. I lose clips when phones change.
- **Excel/Google Sheets** — my "player database". Eleven columns. Last edited 2024.
- **A 1TB external drive** — match footage, mostly unlabeled.
- **A Google Drive folder I share with Rajah and the assistant coaches** — the shortlist, weekly.
- **Wyscout** — for senior-team prep, not grassroots. ₹20K/year for the individual scout license. Useless for U-15 because U-15 isn't on Wyscout.
- **YouTube** — for highlight reels parents post. Half are over-edited.
- **The phone calls** — the actual scouting infrastructure.
- **My eyes, on the ground** — the only thing I trust without a multiplier.

Everything I do is held together by relationships. The day I retire, eighty percent of my scouting capacity walks out with me. Kerala Blasters know this. So does every other ISL club.

### KPIs

The board doesn't say "scouting KPIs" out loud. What they measure is: how many academy graduates make the senior squad? How many of them are net-positive on transfer value? How many trials produce a signing? Did we miss a name that turned up at FC Goa or Bengaluru? That last one — the _miss_ — is the one that ends careers. Naushad Moosa missed nobody for a decade and that's why he runs U-23 India now.

There is no monthly bonus. There is the implicit promise that if I find a Sandesh Jhingan before anyone else does, I keep my job for five more years.

### The nightmare

I sign a 16-year-old. He's been playing club football since he was 11. Trial measurements look strong — vertical jump 58 cm, beep test 14.2. Six months in I find out the beep test was self-reported, the coach who "verified" the trial was the kid's uncle, and the vertical jump was measured against a wall with a piece of chalk. He's slow, he's small, he can't recover. I cut him after a season. Rajah remembers. The board remembers.

Or worse: I sign him over a kid from Hyderabad who I never saw, who turns up at NEUFC two seasons later and signs a senior contract.

Both nightmares are the same nightmare: **bad data made me sign the wrong player, or invisible data made me miss the right one.**

### The hope

A scout's career has one defining signing. Mine isn't here yet. The hope is straightforward: I want to find a kid in a town I've never been to, watch him play three matches without flying there, see fitness numbers I trust, and put him on a plane to Kochi before any other scout has heard his name.

That's it. Everything else in this document is in service of that sentence.

## 3. The verification multiplier — the killer feature

This is the single thing that separates OnlyKrida from every other piece of sports tech I've been pitched.

The platform applies a confidence multiplier to every fitness number based on **how the data was captured**:

| Tier           | Multiplier | What it means to me                                                 |
| -------------- | ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Self-reported  | 0.7×       | The kid typed it in. Treat as a claim, not a fact.                  |
| App-measured   | 0.85×      | Phone sensors recorded the test. Better than nothing, not great.    |
| Coach-verified | 1.0×       | A registered coach watched the test. This is my baseline for trust. |
| Center-tested  | 1.1×       | Tested at a Khelo India / SAI / accredited centre. Premium signal.  |

Every score on every athlete profile is _adjusted_ by this multiplier before it shows up in a scout's ranking. A self-reported beep test of 14.0 is shown as 9.8-equivalent in scout-ranking views. A center-tested 13.0 is shown as 14.3. **The platform punishes athletes who lie. It rewards athletes who get verified.**

### Why this changes my decision-making

Today, when I get a clip on WhatsApp, I have one job: figure out if the number attached to it is real. That job is 80% of my week. With OnlyKrida, that job is **already done before I open the profile**. The athlete has either:

- Submitted a self-reported number (I see the 0.7× and weight accordingly), or
- Done it on the app with phone sensors (0.85× — phone GPS for sprints, accelerometer for jumps, beep audio with verified completion), or
- Had a registered coach co-sign it (1.0× — and I can see _which_ coach), or
- Been tested at a SAI centre or AIFF-accredited academy (1.1× — non-negotiable trust).

I'm not verifying the number anymore. The platform did. I'm deciding what to do with a number I already trust.

### Why "coach-verified" beats my WhatsApp video

A coach-verified number on OnlyKrida is signed by **a coach with their own profile, their own credentials, their own track record on the platform.** If Rajah Rizwan verified a beep test, his name is attached to it. If a coach I've never heard of verified one, I can click his profile and see: how many athletes has he verified? How many of them moved up zones? Did any of his "Strong" athletes wash out at trials?

The WhatsApp video has a number from someone's uncle. The OnlyKrida number has a chain of accountability. **That's not a feature. That's the entire product.**

### The "I trust this number" moment

The first time I'll feel it: a kid from Vizag pops up on my filter — center-back, 16, app-measured beep test of 12.8, coach-verified vertical jump 54 cm, coach is someone I met at a Sreenidi Deccan event in 2024. Multiplier-adjusted score puts him in the top 7% of his cohort. I message his coach through the app. Coach replies in 20 minutes. Highlight reel is on the profile. I forward it to Rajah with two lines and a screenshot.

I haven't been to Vizag. I have not paid any agent. I have not gone through three layers of WhatsApp. I have a player on the shortlist in 12 minutes.

That's the moment. And once I've had it once, I subscribe.

## 5. Objections + responses

### "I have my own network — why pay for this?"

You have a network of 200 contacts across South India and Goa. India produces approximately 15,000 viable U-19 prospects every year across 28 states. Your network sees 8% of them. OnlyKrida, by Year 2, will surface the other 92% to you in a single query. Your network is not the moat — your _judgement_ is. Stop wasting your judgement on triangulation work. Use the platform to do triangulation; use yourself to make the call.

### "Indian sports tech died with Stupa. What makes you different?"

Stupa was a marketplace for venue bookings. KheloMore is a marketplace for venue bookings. Decathlon Play is a marketplace for venue bookings. **OnlyKrida is not a marketplace.** It is a verified athlete database with a scout-discovery surface. The closest spiritual analog is Tonsser in Europe — 2 million active users, 7 years in. We are India's version, multi-sport from day one, with a verification multiplier no European product has. We are also — critically — owned by an ex-Indian-football-player (Anirudh) who built it because _he_ was the kid in Hyderabad nobody saw. The founder's incentive is correct.

### "How do I know the data isn't gamed?"

Three answers, in order:

1. **The multiplier itself is the answer.** Self-reported data is shown at 0.7× — gaming it gets you nothing because no scout filters on it. The multiplier system makes lying economically irrational.
2. **Coach verifications are accountable.** Every coach has a track record on the platform. If a coach verifies twenty kids who all wash out at trials, their multiplier weight goes down. Bad coaches get filtered out.
3. **Center-tested data comes from places we don't control.** SAI centres, AIFF-accredited academies, Khelo India fitness assessments — when those flow in, they're at 1.1× because we trust the _centre_, not us.

If you find one gamed profile, message me. We bring it down within 24 hours and the coach who verified it loses their badge. That's the deal.

### "I sign players based on intangibles — speed and skill don't capture instinct."

Correct. OnlyKrida does not replace your eye on the ground. It replaces the **list of names you put your eye on**. Today, you walk into a trial with a list of names from WhatsApp. Tomorrow, you walk in with a list of names that have been pre-filtered by physical capability. Your job — judging instinct — doesn't change. You just do less wasted judging.

### "I've been burned by demoware before."

Fair. So here's the deal:

- **First 3 months are free for the first 50 ISL/I-League scouts.** No card on file. Cancel any time.
- **One pilot academy near you** — Sreenidi Deccan, Bengaluru FC, Forca Goa, your call — gets free athlete onboarding so the data is real.
- **No vendor lock-in.** You can export your shortlist as CSV any time.
- **If you don't shortlist 5 verified athletes you'd take to a real trial in your first 90 days, we refund the next year.** That's a written commitment, not a marketing line.

### "Is this just another LinkedIn for athletes?"

LinkedIn for athletes is what it looks like to a parent. To a scout, it's a Wyscout-class search engine with grassroots data nobody else has. The athlete-side product (highlights, stats, social) is the _fuel_. The scout-side product (Discover, Top Matches, Shortlist, AI recommendations, multiplier-adjusted ranking) is the _engine_. You are the engine's customer.

## 6. Pricing & tier psychology

### Indian B2B reality

Anyone pricing this in USD and translating doesn't understand the buyer. ISL clubs pay Wyscout ₹2-3 lakh/year per individual seat. That's the ceiling, and Wyscout gets it because they own European pro data. We are not Wyscout. We are _the only people who have grassroots Indian data_. Different value, different price.

### Recommended structure

**Free tier (Scout)** — gives away the wedge:

- Search Discover with up to 3 filters at once
- View 10 athlete profiles per day
- Shortlist up to 25 athletes
- Basic message templates to athletes

This is the demo on autopilot. 80% of curious scouts never convert past free, and that's fine — they're seeding the network.

**Pro tier** — ₹1,499/month (or ₹14,999/year, with the second month free):

- Unlimited Discover queries with all filters (including verification multiplier)
- Unlimited profile views
- AI Scout Cards (Top Matches with reasoning)
- Direct messaging
- Trend tracking on shortlist
- Export shortlist to CSV

**Club tier** — ₹49,999/year per academy:

- 5 scout seats, unlimited
- Bulk opportunity posting
- Trial logistics (apply / RSVP / track)
- Branded academy profile
- Krida AI for opportunity matching
- Monthly insight report on your region

**Federation tier** — custom pricing, starting ₹2.5 lakh/year:

- All of the above
- Cross-club analytics
- Verified-coach badge issuance
- Co-branded fitness centre integrations

### The pricing model that scouts hate least

"Pay per signing" feels fair until you realise how few signings happen. "Pay for AI matches" feels right but gets gamed by users who run 50 queries to amortise the fee. **Flat subscription wins** because it matches how Wyscout sells, how scouts already think, and how clubs budget annually.

### Annual vs monthly

Indian B2B pays slow but pays once. **Push annual hard.** Annual unlocks the second month free. Monthly is offered only as a bridge for the first quarter — to remove the "I might not use it" objection at signup.

### The anchor

₹1,499/month feels like nothing next to ₹20,000/month for Wyscout. That gap is the point. It positions OnlyKrida as the _complement_ — keep your Wyscout for European data, add OnlyKrida for grassroots. Easier sale than displacement.

If a scout walks away from the demo without internalising the multiplier as a _trust mechanism_ rather than a _score adjustment_, they will not subscribe. Every other objection has a fallback.
