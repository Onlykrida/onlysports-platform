# Marketing Strategy — Athlete Perspective

> Written from inside the head of one specific kid: **Vihaan, 15, Karimnagar/Hyderabad, footballer.**
> Not "athletes". Not "young athletes". Vihaan. Singular. If a thing doesn't move him, it doesn't belong here.

---

## 1. Vihaan in detail

**Name:** Vihaan Reddy. Class 10, Government High School, near Boduppal. Fifteen years old, 5'7", left-footed centre-mid who can also play left-back. School ID number he can quote faster than his Aadhaar.

**Phone:** Realme Narzo 50, his cousin's hand-me-down. 64GB, half full of football reels and one half-finished Pardeep Narpadeep dribbling tutorial he downloaded over school Wi-Fi because mobile data is rationed (₹239/month Jio, dad's plan, shared with mom).

**Family:** Mom runs a tiffin service from the kitchen — three tiers, ₹1,800/month per tier, sixteen regulars. Dad drives an Ola Prime, twelve-hour shifts, mostly airport runs. Older sister is in 2nd year B.Com at a college in Tarnaka, "the smart one." Vihaan is "the football one" — said with affection, not pride. Yet.

**Day in the life (Tuesday):**

- 5:50am — alarm. Mom is already up making sambar. He drinks last night's leftover milk, eats one idli standing up.
- 6:15–7:15am — runs to the LB Nagar maidan, joins the under-17 group his coach Suresh Anna runs. Six other boys. They do passing rondos, then a 7v7. The "ground" is half-cement, half-grass. Two goalposts, one without a net.
- 7:30am — back home, bucket bath, school uniform.
- 8:30am–3:30pm — school. He is bored. Maths teacher likes him because he is the first to volunteer to clean the board. Nobody at school knows he plays.
- 4:00–6:30pm — second session at LB Nagar or Saroornagar Stadium when Suresh Anna can swing a session there. Sometimes a futsal scratch game on the cement court at New Hyd Public School if his classmate Aarav's brother is around to unlock the gate.
- 7:30pm — dinner. He scrolls Instagram. Watches: Sunil Chhetri pinned post (he has watched the "Please come watch us" video 14 times, knows the cadence by heart), Sreenidi Deccan FC reels, a creator called Football Lemon, two random "Football Trials Hyderabad" reels with WhatsApp numbers in the caption. He saves all of them. He has DM'd two of those numbers. One blue-tick replied with "send video on this number". He sent three clips. Nothing came back. That was four months ago.
- 9:30pm — homework, badly. Phone face-down on his Mathematics textbook so the screen glow doesn't wake his sister.
- 10:45pm — one last scroll. Sleeps with the phone under his pillow because the alarm is on it.

**His dream — specific, not generic:** He wants to play for **Sreenidi Deccan FC's reserves**. He knows Hyderabad FC is gone — moved to Delhi as Sporting Club Delhi in October — and he is quietly furious about it because his older self had imagined being scouted at the Gachibowli stadium HFC used. So now: Sreenidi Deccan, the Hyderabad I-League side. Or — a longer shot but the one he tells nobody — **Bengaluru FC's residential academy in Bellary**. He has researched the location. It's eight hours by bus.

**Path to that dream today (the brutal version):** The scouts who pick the Sreenidi Deccan U-19 are people who are friends with people who know Suresh Anna. Suresh Anna is good — he played second-division football in the late 90s — but his network ends at HFL B-Division. A scout from Bengaluru FC will physically never come to LB Nagar. Vihaan's only path today is: (a) Suresh Anna's coach in Vijayawada knows somebody who knows the Sreenidi U-17 selection committee, and (b) Subroto Cup zonal qualifier in August, if his school enters a team, and Vihaan is class 10 so he has one year left. **A scout in Mumbai watching Bengaluru FC's reserves does not have a button to click that surfaces him.** Geography is the gate.

**His parents:**

- **Mom** is not against football, but every quarterly fee reminder ("₹1,400 for Suresh Anna's training, paid in three instalments") is a small fight. She wants him to be like his sister — predictable, B.Com, government job.
- **Dad** secretly likes that his son plays. He used to play tennis-ball cricket. He bought Vihaan his first proper boots (Nivia Carbonite, ₹2,200, big purchase). But his dad will never call himself a "football dad" — he is too tired.
- They are not anti-app. They are anti-distraction. The first time Vihaan installs OnlyKrida and shows them a real scout from a real club has viewed his profile, his mom's posture changes. That moment is the point of this product for the parental layer.

## 2. "Why now" + "Why this app"

**The single sentence Vihaan says to his teammate Aarav, in the LB Nagar dugout, on the day he downloads:**

> "Bey, isme actual scouts hain — Sreenidi waale bhi search karte hain. Profile bana, free hai, bas video upload karna hai."

(Translation, but the original is what he'd actually say.)

**The 3 things he can do in OnlyKrida that he literally cannot do anywhere else today:**

1. **Have a scout from a club he has never been to actually find him by filtering "Centre-mid, U-17, Hyderabad, left-footed, Rising fitness zone."** Instagram has no such filter. WhatsApp has no such filter. Suresh Anna's network has no such filter. This is the Hyderabad test made into a UI.

2. **Run a phone-recorded Yo-Yo IR1 and get an `app_measured` verification badge** that follows him into a scout's search — verified at 0.85× confidence, but verified, which is more than any reel on Instagram has ever been. Then, when Suresh Anna verifies it next week, it jumps to `coach_verified` 1.0× and he sees the multiplier change. He keeps doing tests because the badge moves.

3. **See, by name, that "Naveen Kumar — Sreenidi Deccan U-19 selector — viewed your profile 2 hours ago."** Not "someone viewed your profile." A named human at a named club. Instagram's "Profile views: 23" is a vanity metric. This is a directed signal.

**What he was doing to get noticed before — brutally honest:**

- Posting Instagram reels of his free kicks. 200–400 views each. His best reel got 1,100 views and 14 likes — three of those likes were his cousins. None of those views were from a scout.
- DM'ing six WhatsApp numbers from "Hyderabad Football Trials" reels. Two ghosted, three sent template forms (one was clearly a paid scam — "₹2,500 for trial registration"), one was Real Sreenidi (he thinks) and never responded after the third clip.
- Submitted one Subroto Cup form through the school sports teacher — only because his school applied, not because he applied himself.
- Walked up to a Sreenidi Deccan first-team player at Gachibowli stadium after a match, said "sir, mein football khelta hoon" — got a nod and a "keep training, beta." That nod replays in his head when he is tired.

**The aha moment:** The first time a real scout's name shows up in his "Profile Viewers" list. That's it. That's the entire product distilled into one notification. He screenshots it. Sends it to Aarav on WhatsApp with three skull emojis. Aarav downloads in ten minutes.

## 3. Killer use cases (ranked by emotional pull)

### #1 — "A scout from a club I've never been to just viewed me"

**Title:** _"Naveen sir from Sreenidi just saw me."_

**Vihaan's internal monologue, 7:42pm, scrolling after dinner:**

> "Bhai. Bhai. Bhai. BHAI. _(zooms in on the name three times)_ Yeh real hai? Yeh actually real Sreenidi waala hai? _(taps to expand profile)_ Haan haan haan badge hai, verified scout. He saw me. He saw the Yo-Yo video. _(checks profile completion)_ I need to add the goal compilation. Now. Phone ke andar phone ka video kaise upload karu — Aarav, video bhej idhar."

**Feature/screen:** `ProfileViewers` component (the "Who Viewed Your Profile" tile on the athlete home, documented as the #1 engagement driver). Each viewer is a real `profiles` row joined to `profile_views` with role + verified badge. When a `scout` role views, push notification fires immediately: _"Naveen Kumar (Sreenidi Deccan FC) just looked at your profile. Make sure your highlight reel is up to date."_

**Why this carries 90+ days:** Once he has experienced one named-scout view, every subsequent push is potentially the next one. He opens the app three times a day to check. On days where a scout actually viewed, he uploads more. On days nobody viewed, he completes his profile to fix that. Engagement loop closes around dopamine + agency.

### #2 — "I went from Building to Rising. My zone moved."

**Title:** _"Bey, mein Rising mein hoon ab."_

**Vihaan's internal monologue, after finishing his Yo-Yo IR1 attempt at LB Nagar with two friends counting beeps for him:**

> "Level 18.4. Pichle mahine 16.2 tha. _(opens app, sees the share card auto-generated)_ Building se Rising. _(stares at the green Rising badge)_ Aarav ko bhejte hain. WhatsApp status pe daalta hoon. Chhetri bhi Rising se start kiya hoga shayad."

**Feature/screen:** Yo-Yo IR1 in-app test (`app/beep-test-live.tsx` + `useSensorRecording`), result writes to `fitness_test_results` with `verification_tier='app_measured'` (0.85× multiplier). Zone calculation in `fitness-test-context.tsx` bumps him from `Building` to `Rising`. Auto-generated share card (loop #5 from the GTM playbook) — image with his name, Rising badge, IR1 level 18.4, and `onlykrida.com/vihaan-reddy`. One-tap share to WhatsApp Status.

**Why this carries 90+ days:** Zone progress is a Strava-style ratchet. Every test that bumps him forward triggers a new share. Every test that doesn't bump him forward makes him want to retest. Coach-verification (1.0× multiplier) is the next dopamine layer — Suresh Anna verifies, the score visibly increases in the scout-search ranking. He never stops chasing the next zone because the words themselves — _Rising → Strong → Elite → Unstoppable_ — are designed to never feel finished.

### #3 — "Sreenidi U-19 trials, June 14. I just applied."

**Title:** _"Trials post mila, form bhar diya."_

**Vihaan's internal monologue, opening the Opportunities tab after a notification ping:**

> "Sreenidi Deccan U-19 Trials — June 14, Gachibowli. _(reads requirements)_ Birth certificate, Aadhaar, last fitness score from the app — wait, wait, wait, last fitness score from the app means **my** Yo-Yo. They want **my** Yo-Yo from this app. _(form auto-filled)_ Bas submit? Bas? Aarav, Aarav, dekh idhar."

**Feature/screen:** Opportunities feed (`opportunities` table + opportunities-context). Sreenidi posts an `opportunity` with `type='trial'`. Vihaan's profile is pre-filled into the application form including his most recent `fitness_test_result` and `verification_tier`. He clicks Apply. An `application` row is created. Sreenidi's scout sees it in their dashboard, alongside 200 other applicants, with a sortable column for verified Yo-Yo + zone.

**Why this carries 90+ days:** Trial cycles are seasonal. May = Subroto zonals. June = Sreenidi U-19. August = Subroto national. October = ISL academy intakes. Every two months a new opportunity drops in his city or one bus ride away — the app becomes the bulletin board he checks reflexively. Critical: _applying through OnlyKrida is fundamentally different from sending a WhatsApp video_, because the receiving scout sees verified data, not a clip in a void.

## 4. Objections + responses

**"Why not just Instagram?"**

> _"Instagram pe scout kaise dhoondega bey? Hashtag pe? 200 reel hote hain har din. Yahaan filter hai — U-17 + Hyderabad + centre-mid. Mein search result mein dikhta hoon. Instagram pe nahi dikhta."_

Reply pattern in copy: "Instagram tells your friends. OnlyKrida tells scouts."

**"I tested — nobody saw it. So what?"**

> _"Tested kya kiya bey? Reel daala? Yeh test alag hai — verified, app measured, zone milta hai. Aur scout ki search mein zone se sort hota hai. Reel sort nahi hota."_

Reply pattern: 4-tier verification badges (`self_reported` → `app_measured` → `coach_verified` → `center_tested`) explained in 30 seconds with a visual ladder. He sees that being `coach_verified` (1.0×) puts him 1.4× higher in scout search ranking than being `self_reported` (0.7×). The math is in the UI.

**"Why should I trust this random app over my coach?"**

> _"Suresh Anna ke saath hi use karna hai bey. Anna ko coach role milta hai, woh tujhe verify karta hai, score 1.0× ho jaata hai. App Anna ko replace nahi kar raha — Anna ke saath kaam karta hai."_

This is critical: OnlyKrida does NOT compete with the coach. The coach becomes a verifier and gets credit (`verified_by_coach_id` on the test result). When Vihaan gets scouted, Suresh Anna's name appears in the scout's view as the verifier — the coach is the hero, not bypassed.

**"Will scouts really see me from Hyderabad?"**

> _"Bro, scout Mumbai mein baitha hai, woh search karta hai 'Hyderabad U-17 centre-mid'. Mein result mein hoon. He clicks. He sees my Yo-Yo. He sees my goals reel. He DM-s me. Yeh hua hai. Naveen sir Sreenidi se already saw — screenshot dekh."_

Marketing artefact: a real testimonial from one of the first 50 closed-beta athletes whose Mumbai-based scout actually contacted them. Without that artefact this objection is unfalsifiable; with it, it's the strongest social proof we have.

**"What if scouts judge me badly?"**

> _"Judge nahi karte bey. Zones hain — Starter, Building, Rising, Strong, Elite, Unstoppable. Koi 'fail' label nahi hai. Building zone mein hoon? Toh scout ko pata hai woh kya dekh raha hai. Mein abhi class 10 hoon — woh dekhega 'Rising potential' nahi 'beginner'."_

This is the never-demotivate principle made into a sales line. The zone names ARE the objection-killer. We never had to write defensive copy because the labels themselves are non-judgmental.

**"My English isn't good — can I use this?"**

> _"Bhai, app Hindi-Telugu-English mix hai. 'Apply karo' button hai. 'Test do' button hai. Tu jab Yo-Yo karta hai, Suresh Anna ki voice me beep play hota hai. Tu DM mein Hindi mein likhta hai, scout English mein padhta hai — auto-translate hai."_

Action item for product: ensure language switcher is prominent on first launch + localised microcopy across the fitness test screens (this is currently English-leaning — flag for Wave 2).

## 5. The viral loop

**When does Vihaan tell Aarav about OnlyKrida?**

The first scout view. Specifically: 7:42pm Tuesday, after dinner, sitting on the building staircase outside his flat because the Wi-Fi signal is better there. Push notification fires: "Naveen Kumar from Sreenidi Deccan FC just viewed your profile." He stares at it for 90 seconds. Then he screenshots it. Then he opens WhatsApp, finds Aarav at the top of his "frequently chatted" list, sends:

```
[screenshot]
bro
BRO
sreenidi waala
real waala
download kar abhi → onlykrida.com/v
```

**What does he show Aarav?** The screenshot. Specifically the _named_ scout view — Naveen Kumar, with the verified-scout badge, with the club name. That single rectangle is the entire product pitch in Vihaan's hand.

**The moment Aarav downloads:** Aarav is already on his phone (he is 15, of course he is). He sees the screenshot, sees the Sreenidi badge, opens the link. The deep-link dropps him into Vihaan's profile preview, with a "Sign up — appear in scout searches like Vihaan" CTA. He signs up, taps role = athlete, picks football, picks Hyderabad. Profile is at 30% complete in 90 seconds. He runs his first Yo-Yo within the week because Vihaan tells him "tu Building mein hai, mein Rising mein, race kar."

**One specific friction point that kills the loop today:** Profile completion takes too long for a 15-year-old's attention span if we don't aggressively pre-fill. If Aarav drops to 30% complete and never reaches 60%, his profile is invisible to scout searches (the scout filter typically requires `profile_completeness >= 60`). He never gets a scout view. He never has anything to screenshot. The loop dies in 7 days. **Fix:** profile-onboarding flow must reach 60% in ≤5 minutes via smart defaults from sport+role+city+age. (This is loop #2 from the GTM playbook; instrumentation already specced.)

- Below: which sections of his profile the scout dwelled on (highlight #2 — the free kick goal vs Glendale; Yo-Yo IR1 verified result; left-foot tag)

**What is in his hand when this happens:**

His phone, propped against the cement wall of the staircase landing outside flat 304. The light from the screen lights up his face. His mother's tiffin steamer is hissing in the kitchen behind him. His sister is at the dining table doing income tax homework. The TV is on Star Maa. None of it registers. He is on the staircase, looking at a rectangle that says a man in a city he has never been to has just decided he is worth tracking. The next thing he does is screenshot it and send it to Aarav. The thing after that is to walk back into the kitchen and show his mom — wordlessly, just the screen — and watch her face change.

**That is the product. Everything else is in service of producing that moment, once a week, for one Vihaan, in one apartment block, in one tier-2 city. We make it happen 50 times a week by month 3, 200 times a week by month 6.**

## Closing principle

Vihaan does not need motivation. He has motivation. He runs at 6am on a half-cement ground for free. What he needs is a button that connects his motivation to a scout's screen. That button is the entire product.

Everything in the marketing — the Instagram ad, the WhatsApp share, the app store copy, the onboarding, the zone names, the verification tiers, the named-scout viewer feed — is one thing said many ways: **"There is a button, and it works."**
