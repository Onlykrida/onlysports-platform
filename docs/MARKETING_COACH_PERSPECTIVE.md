## 1. My day, in case you've never seen one

I leave home at 3:45pm. The auto to LB Nagar takes 30 minutes if there's no rain. I'm on the
ground at 4:15, fifteen minutes before the boys, because if I'm not there first they think
they don't have to be either. I unload the cones from the storeroom. I fill three jerry cans
from the tap behind the canteen because the water near the pitch hasn't worked since 2024.

The U-12s come first — 28 boys, half of them barefoot when they arrive. We do warm-ups,
ball-mastery, a 7v7. The U-15s and U-17s arrive at 5:30, by which time I'm already coaching
two age groups simultaneously. My assistant Ravi handles the U-12s' last 30 minutes while
I run the older groups through tactical work. I'm watching Akhil, the U-17 left-back —
he's the one I think can play I-League. I'm watching Vihaan, U-15 — quickest kid on the
ground but he plays with his head down. I'm watching Roshan, U-13 — too small for his
age but his first touch is unteachable.

By 7:30 the boys are gone. By 8:00 I'm replying to parents on WhatsApp — three groups,
182 numbers between them. "Sir, when is the match this Sunday?" "Sir, my son's fees are
delayed, two more weeks please." "Sir, can you write a letter for school admission, my
boy is good in football."

I write letters on academy letterhead. I file attendance in an Excel sheet a former student
made me in 2019. I send my best three boys' video clips to the Sreenidi Deccan U-17 coach
on WhatsApp every six months and pray he watches them. I have never heard back from a
Mumbai or Bengaluru scout in my 28 years. Not once. I don't know any.

So when somebody tells me there's an app for scouts — my honest first reaction is, **another
inbox**. I have four already. I am not adding a fifth unless it does something my four cannot.

The three boys I lose sleep over are Akhil, Vihaan, and Roshan. The other 57 boys, I want
the best for them too — they are learning football, learning discipline, getting away from
phones — but they're not the three. The three are why I'm still doing this at 52.

## 2. What `coach_verified` actually means to me

Here is what I understood when Anirudh sat me down at the canteen and explained it.

The OnlyKrida app has four levels of how a kid's stats are believed:

- A boy types his own 40m time into the app. Some of it is bullshit. Scout cuts that number.
- The boy runs the test inside the OnlyKrida app and the phone records it via sensors. Closer
  to true.
- **I**, Coach Suresh, watch him do it — either standing on the pitch with my own eyes, or
  reviewing his video on my phone later — and I tap a button that says **"verified by coach."**
  The scout treats that number as **the truth.**
- A government-approved testing centre runs him through it. Best of all but rare; my boys
  will never see one of those.

Out of those four, **the third one is the one I control.** That is my level. That is where
my reputation enters the platform.

When Anirudh said _"the scout multiplies your verification weight by 1.0, the highest realistic
tier most athletes will hit"_ — what he actually said to me, in my language, was: **the scout
in Mumbai will believe Akhil's 40m time because I, Coach Suresh, said it was true.** That is
me, vouching, in writing, in front of the entire scout network in India.

That is also a thing I've been doing for 28 years on academy letterhead — but on letterhead,
my name reaches one scout. On OnlyKrida, my name and my "Verified 12 athletes" count reach
every scout looking at any of my boys' profiles. **The letterhead reaches one. The platform
reaches everyone.** That is the upgrade.

### The two modes — when I pick which

OnlyKrida gives me two ways to verify.

**In-person mode**: The boy runs the test on the pitch. I'm standing right there with my
phone. The app's stopwatch is going. He runs. I tap "verified — in person." Takes 30 seconds.

**Video review mode**: The boy filmed his test at home or at school, uploads it to OnlyKrida,
and the video shows up on my phone in a queue. I watch it on my couch after dinner. If the
form is clean and the result is real, I tap "verified — video reviewed." If the kid cheated —
ran from the wrong line, false-started, used a downhill — I tap "rejected" with a one-line
note.

When do I pick which? Easy. **The boys at LB Nagar I see every day, I verify in person —
faster, no doubt about anything.** The boys from outside my academy who want a coach's
verification — say a kid from Karimnagar whose own school PE teacher won't sign off — they
send me video, I review on my phone in 10 minutes, and I either bless it or kick it back.
That second mode is also how I keep my standards visible: my "Verified 12 athletes,
Rejected 4" stat tells scouts I am not a rubber stamp.

The video-review mode shipped yesterday. Until then, I had to drive to wherever the kid
was. Now I don't. **That is the difference between this being a part-time job and being a
ten-minute-a-day habit.**

### Why this beats my letterhead

Three reasons.

1. **The letterhead reaches one scout. The platform reaches every scout.**
2. **The letterhead is words. The verification is words attached to a measured number** —
   a 40m sprint, a Yo-Yo level, a vertical jump. Numbers don't argue.
3. **My letterhead has my signature; my OnlyKrida verification has my track record.** Six
   months in, every coach has a number — "Verified 12, Rejected 4, success rate of recommended
   athletes who got trials: 8 of 12." That number is my professional reputation, made
   public. It compounds. Letterheads don't compound.

## 4. The killer use cases

### Use case #1 — Get my best 3 boys in front of Sporting Club Delhi and Sreenidi Deccan scouts in a week

**Before**: I'd record videos of Akhil, our left-back, on my Redmi phone. I'd send them to
two contacts via WhatsApp — one at Sreenidi Deccan, one through a former teammate who
played for Salgaocar. I'd hear back 1 time in 10. The video gets lost in the scout's chat
backlog. Akhil doesn't know it ever went anywhere.

**After**: I open the OnlyKrida app. I run Akhil through three fitness tests on the pitch
in 20 minutes — Yo-Yo IR1, 40m sprint, vertical jump. I in-person-verify each. His profile
is now showing him in the **Strong** zone, with a coach-verified tag, and a 12-second
highlight reel I uploaded last week. Sreenidi Deccan's scout has set a saved search for
"Telangana, U-17, left-back, Strong+ fitness, coach-verified or higher" — Akhil shows up
on Monday morning. Sporting Club Delhi's scout, who covers the south for U-17 trials, has
the same alert. Akhil gets a "We'd like to see you at our Hyderabad open trial" message in
the app on Wednesday.

I did 30 seconds of work per test. Akhil has been seen by two ISL-tier clubs. **I have
never been able to do this.**

**Screen I spend most time on**: The **Coach Verification Queue** card on my home screen —
the badge tells me how many videos are waiting for my review. I clear the queue every
night after dinner.

**Athlete outcome**: Akhil gets the trial invitation. The trial leads to a U-17 academy
contract or it doesn't — but he was _seen_. That is the part I could never deliver.

### Use case #2 — Find the kid I haven't met yet

**Before**: A boy from Vikarabad, two hours outside Hyderabad, has nobody to vouch for him.
His PE teacher doesn't know what a Yo-Yo test is. His family can't afford to bring him to
LB Nagar to train under me.

**After**: He uploads an `app_measured` Yo-Yo result through OnlyKrida — verified by the
phone's sensors. The system flags him to coaches in his region as "this kid is in the
Rising zone but has zero coach verification." I see him in my **"Athletes near you needing
coach review"** list. I review his sprint video on my phone Tuesday evening. If it's clean,
I verify it from 80km away. If it's good enough, I message him — _"come run a session
with us at LB Nagar, I want to see you in person."_

That kid — who would have been invisible his whole life — is now in my pipeline because the
app told me he existed. **The app finds talent for me, not just for the scout.**

**Screen I spend most time on**: Athlete profiles when I'm reviewing video, and the
in-app DM to invite a kid I want to see in person.

**Athlete outcome**: A Vikarabad kid I would never have met gets a coach who can vouch for
him. Without the app, his 40m time stays self-reported and the Mumbai scout discounts it
30%. With my verification, he hits the same scout-search that Akhil does.

### Use case #3 — Stop writing the same school-admission letter 40 times a year

**Before**: 40 times a year — I am not exaggerating — a parent asks me to write a "sports
quota admission" letter for their boy, on academy letterhead, addressed to the school's
principal. Each one takes 15 minutes. 40 × 15 = 10 hours of unpaid administrative work
every year.

**After**: I tap "Generate verified report" on the boy's OnlyKrida profile. It produces a
PDF with his fitness zone, his coach-verified test results, his attendance with my academy
(I can plug that in eventually), and my signature on the verification. Parent forwards it
to the school. Done. Counts as my official recommendation.

**Screen I spend most time on**: The athlete profile's "Generate report" button.

**Athlete outcome**: Faster admissions, fewer late-night letters, and a PDF that is more
credible than my letterhead because it has data behind it. The parent treats me like a
professional, not a peon.

## 5. The objections — the actual ones I have

**"I'm an old-school coach. Kids should earn it the hard way."**

I tell my boys: the hard way is the training. The discipline. The 5:30am runs. The
hard way is what builds the player. **The discovery is not the hard way — it's the unfair
way, and right now it's unfair against my boys.** A Mumbai academy boy gets seen by
default. My Hyderabad boy has to be lucky. The app does not make football softer for the
kid. It only makes the _discovery_ fairer.

**"I'm not getting paid to verify. Why should I do it?"**

Direct. I'm not paid to write letters either, and I write 40 a year. I do this work because
it's the only way my boys have a future. OnlyKrida makes the same work _visible_: my
"Verified 12 athletes, 4 of them got trials, 1 of them signed" stat sits on my profile.
That is reputation. Reputation gets me three things — more parents bringing their kids to
LB Nagar (₹500–₹2,000 a month per kid; 10 new kids covers a year of my time), a possible
academy partnership with a club like Sreenidi Deccan, and the only retirement plan I have.
I'd ask Anirudh to consider a small finder's fee — say ₹5,000 — when a boy I verified gets
signed by an ISL-tier club. That would seal it. But honestly, the reputation alone is worth
the 10 minutes.

**"What if I verify a kid who's actually faking? My reputation is on the line."**

It is. That's the point of the system — and that's why I take it seriously. I do not
verify what I have not seen. If the video is shaky, if the start line is off, if the kid
looks like he's running 35m and calling it 40m, I reject. Every coach develops a track
record on the platform. The bad coaches get exposed in 6 months when their verified athletes
fail trial fitness tests. The good coaches' verifications get even more weight. The system
is self-cleaning. **I'm not worried about my reputation — I'm worried about the platform
filling up with rubber-stamp coaches who hurt mine.** That's a question for Anirudh.

**"I run a private academy. Won't this make scouts skip the academy and go direct to my kids?"**

Honest answer: yes, the scout _could_ skip me. But two things stop them. One — they need
my _verification_ to trust the data, so I'm structurally in the loop. Two — when the kid
gets the trial, the kid's profile says "coach-verified by Coach Suresh, LB Nagar Academy."
Every scout learns that the boys who succeed went through _me_. That is brand. That is how
LB Nagar Academy becomes a known feeder, the same way Minerva and TFA are known feeders.
The app is **how my academy's reputation goes from local to national.** Scouts skipping
the academy is the old fear. The new reality is the academy _getting credit_ on every
profile of every kid that came through it.

**"There are 200 athletes in my school. I have 30 minutes a day."**

That's not me, that's the PE teachers. Different use case — see section 7.

**"How does this not just become more notification spam?"**

Simple — I turn on only two notifications: (1) "a boy from my academy has a video waiting
for verification" and (2) "a scout has expressed interest in a boy I verified." Everything
else off. Anirudh said the app respects this. If they break that, I uninstall. That's the
deal.

## 6. The "academy in the loop" question

This is the part Anirudh keeps asking me. _Is the coach the bridge or the bottleneck?_

The honest answer: **OnlyKrida lets the kid go direct, and the coach has to choose to be
useful.** If I sit out, my boys still get scouted — just at the `app_measured` 0.85×
multiplier instead of my `coach_verified` 1.0×. They lose a small edge. They still get seen.
**The platform does not need me. But my boys do better with me. So I engage.**

That is the right design. A platform that _forced_ the coach to be in the loop would be
a platform that fails the Hyderabad boy whose PE teacher can't be bothered. The platform's
soul is the kid. **I am earning my place in the loop, every time, by adding a multiplier
worth earning.**

What pulls a coach in:

1. **Reputation visible at scale.** "Verified 12, 4 trials, 1 signed" is my new CV.
2. **Pipeline of new athletes who DM me asking for verification.** Free leads.
3. **A finder's bonus** if my verified athlete signs a professional contract — even ₹5,000
   to ₹25,000 per signing would be transformative for me. **This is the single thing that
   would convert me from "engaged" to "evangelist."**
4. **Academy branding** — every kid I verify carries my academy's name on their profile
   forever. Local academy → known feeder → premium fees → better infrastructure. That is
   a 10-year arc and it starts with this app.

If OnlyKrida doesn't ship #3 — the finder's bonus — they will get the engaged coaches but
they will not get the evangelists. I would tell Anirudh that.

## 7. The PE teacher use case — different beast

A government school PE teacher in Telangana has 200 kids and 30 minutes a day. He cannot
be Coach Suresh. He needs a stripped-down version of OnlyKrida.

What works for him:

1. **Bulk fitness-test mode** — he gathers all 200 boys for one period a week, runs them
   through one drill (say, the 40m sprint), and the app records the entire class in a single
   tap-to-time flow. 30 minutes for 200 kids' results to enter the platform. App-measured,
   not coach-verified — but the teacher is _attesting_ (one tap) that he was on the ground
   while it happened. That gets the kid to the `coach_verified` tier, just at a lighter
   touch.
2. **One-tap "this kid is special"** — at the end of the period, the teacher flags 2 kids
   out of the 200 as "I'd like a real coach to look at this kid." That sends an alert to
   the nearest academy coach (me, in LB Nagar). I review their video. If I agree, I message
   the kid. The PE teacher becomes a _spotter_. Coach Suresh becomes the _verifier_. The
   work is split.
3. **No DMs, no parents, no scout interaction.** PE teacher does not become a recruiter. He
   just clears 2 kids a week into the academy-coach pipeline. The platform handles the rest.

Five minutes a week. Two kids flagged. Over a school year, 80 kids surfaced. From 200,000
PE teachers in India, **that is the entire grassroots layer of the country flowing into
the platform without anyone working harder than they already do.** That is the moonshot.

channel in every Tier-2 city in India.** That decision is the lever.
