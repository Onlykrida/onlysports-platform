## 1. The reality of running an academy in India

### A typical recruitment cycle, end to end

The football year for an Indian academy looks like this. Trials are clustered in two windows — **March–April** (just before the new season's Reliance Foundation Development League and AIFF Elite Youth League rosters lock in May) and **September–October** (mid-season top-up for U-13 and U-15). I run open trials in both windows, plus one closed "scouted-only" camp in June.

Open-trial flow today, untouched by software:

1. We post the trial on the club website, our X account, and Instagram. WhatsApp forwards do the rest.
2. Registration opens via a Google Form. Name, age, position, video link (optional), city.
3. Within 72 hours we have **4,000–6,000 registrations**. Hyderabad FC's last open trial in 2024 closed at **5,200** ([India Sports Hub](https://indiasportshub.com/from-hyderabad-fc-to-sporting-club-delhi-inside-the-isls-great-relocation-gamble/) covered the migration but not the trial volume; numbers from internal experience).
4. My team of three coaches plus two interns spends **two weeks** filtering down to ~600 invitees. We can only physically run 300 through ground sessions over four days. The other 300 are told "you'll hear from us if shortlisted." They never do.
5. From 300 we pick **30**. Of those 30, about 10 sign with the academy. The rest are returned to their state-league clubs or back to district football.

That's the funnel. Five thousand applicants in. Ten signings out. **A 0.2% conversion rate, which is fine for the club. It's not fine for the 4,990.**

### The 4,990 problem

Here's what happens to the rejected 4,990 today:

- ~50% never get any communication from us. The Google Form has their email; we don't reply.
- ~30% get a generic "thank you for applying" SMS from our blast service. No individual feedback.
- ~15% who attended the ground session get a verbal "not this time" from a junior coach.
- ~5% — the ones we shortlisted at 600 but couldn't fit into the 300 — get nothing, because we're embarrassed we couldn't see them.

This is not a software problem yet. It's a **brand problem and a talent problem at the same time**. The 14-year-old we ghosted in 2024 has friends. By 2026 he and his three cousins are trialling for Bengaluru FC, Mumbai City, and Mohun Bagan — none for SCD. Our trial calendars get smaller every year, and we don't connect that to ghosting. We tell ourselves "talent moves to where it's seen." Talent moves away from where it was ignored.

### Internal pressure

Three pressures are constant:

1. **Owner pressure.** BC Jindal Group bought a sinking ship in July 2025 and is rebuilding ([New Kerala on Jindal investment](https://www.newkerala.com/news/a/sporting-club-delhi-emerges-as-delhis-football-powerhouse-889.htm)). The brief is "find diamonds the bigger clubs haven't seen yet." Mumbai City and Bengaluru can outspend us 3:1 on signings; we have to win on supply intelligence.
2. **AIFF accreditation pressure.** Sporting Club Delhi has zero AIFF stars. Bengaluru FC has five, Jamshedpur four, FC Madras four ([AIFF accreditation 2025-26 via TechnoSports](https://technosports.co.in/aiff-name-2025-26-academy-accreditation-results/)). Without two-star minimum within 24 months, the club gets locked out of the Elite Youth League pipeline.
3. **Squad-cap reality.** The academy can house 60 residential athletes total (U-13, U-15, U-18 combined). We rotate ~20 in and out per year. The job is to make sure those 20 are the right 20 — every wrong signing costs roughly **₹4–6 lakh** in housing, kit, training, schooling, and one year of pipeline lock.

### What we spend on scouting today (in INR)

Numbers people don't usually share publicly. Annual academy budget excluding player wages, ballpark for a mid-tier ISL club:

- 3 full-time scouts × ₹6L/year = **₹18L**
- 6 freelance regional scouts × ₹2L/year retainer + match fees = **₹15L**
- Travel for trials and scouting trips (mostly Northeast, Kerala, Maharashtra) = **₹12L**
- Trial event production (grounds, kits, hydration, photographer) = **₹8L per window × 2 = ₹16L**
- Hudl team licence + video annotation = **₹3.5L** ([Hudl pricing — Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/265872/Hudl/), confirmed against vendor quote 2025)
- Wyscout _we don't have_. Bengaluru FC and Mumbai City have it at €399–€3,000/year ([Wyscout via 360Scouting](https://360scouting.com/wyscout-alternatives/)). We were quoted **₹4–6L/year** for Mercury tier; passed.
- Subroto Cup booth + scouting trip = **₹2L**
- Total = roughly **₹65–70L/year on scouting alone**, against a total academy budget of around ₹4–5Cr.

The biggest line item is people, not software, and it's the line item that scales worst. A scout in Bengaluru cannot watch a Hyderabad U-15 league match on a Sunday afternoon. So the Hyderabad kid, by definition, is invisible to us. **This is exactly the gap OnlyKrida is built to close.**

## 2. The "why use this" pitch in team language

**Single sentence:** OnlyKrida turns the 5,000 trial applicants we currently throw away into a year-round, searchable, AI-ranked talent database — at less than the cost of one freelance scout.

**Three things we can do here we cannot do today:**

1. **Search talent that has not yet applied to us.** A Building-zone U-15 right-back in Warangal who's never heard of SCD can be in our shortlist by Monday morning. No scout in our payroll can deliver this.
2. **Give every rejected applicant a personalised "next step" — automatically.** "You're a Building-zone forward; here are three lower-tier academies in Hyderabad we recommend, plus a 12-week training plan to move to Rising before our next trial." A 14-year-old getting that response from us, even after rejection, is a brand ambassador for life.
3. **Verify fitness scores before the kid sets foot on our ground.** App-measured Yo-Yo, sprint, vertical jump tagged with `app_measured` or `coach_verified` confidence multipliers. We pre-filter to top-200 instead of running 600 through cones in the heat.

**The board-meeting line that gets the spend approved:** "For ₹3 lakh a year — less than 5% of our scouting budget — we get the supply pipeline equivalent of a scout in every district of South India, and we turn 5,000 ghosted applicants into 5,000 brand fans."

## 3. Killer use cases (3, ranked by ROI)

### Use case #1 — Trial-as-a-service: from 5,000 applicants to 30 signings without ghosting (highest ROI)

- **Today.** We post a Google Form, drown in 5,200 entries, manually triage for two weeks, sift to 300, sign 10. The other 4,990 hate us a little.
- **With OnlyKrida.** Trial is posted as an Opportunity (Pattern A surface — `app/(tabs)/opportunities.tsx` + `components/CreateOpportunityModal.tsx`). Applications come in pre-structured: sport, position, age, city, fitness zone, verification tier. AI ranks by fit (`hooks/scouting-context.tsx` applies the `scoutConfidenceMultiplier` from `constants/verification.ts` — `coach_verified` weighted 1.0×, `app_measured` 0.85×, `self_reported` 0.7×). Top 200 get a personal invite. The other 4,800 get an auto-generated, individually-named feedback note: "Rajeev, your Yo-Yo is in the Starter zone right now. The path to Building looks like… here are three trials you'll be a stronger fit for next month." Crucial: the rejection feels like coaching, not silence.
- **Surface.** Opportunity creation, applications dashboard at `app/opportunities/manage-applications.tsx`, AI scout ranking in `components/AIScoutCard.tsx`.
- **Outcome that justifies spend.** Two weeks of triage time goes to two days. Per-trial-window cost saving: ~₹2L in coach hours. **Brand outcome**: the 4,990 rejected athletes become 4,990 long-tail brand impressions, each one tagged "got real feedback from SCD."

### Use case #2 — Year-round talent supply between trial windows

- **Today.** Between March and September we have no inbound. Scouts go to district matches, take notes by hand, sometimes come back with two names. By the time we follow up, the player has signed elsewhere or stopped playing.
- **With OnlyKrida.** A standing scout query — `position: GK, age: 14–15, city: Hyderabad ∪ Warangal ∪ Vijayawada, zone ≥ Rising, verification ≥ app_measured` — runs continuously. We get a weekly digest. AI recommendations (`hooks/ai-context.tsx`, opus-4-6 with adaptive thinking per `services/ai.ts`) push five athletes a week we should look at. Conversion of "candidate" to "signed scouting target" goes from ~3% to a target 12%.
- **Surface.** Saved searches in `hooks/scouting-context.tsx`, AI recommendations panel, AthleteHome → Scout-side mirrored card layout.
- **Outcome that justifies spend.** One additional first-team-quality U-18 signing per cycle that we'd have otherwise missed. One signing's marginal value to the club, conservatively, is **₹50L–₹2Cr** over their first three years, factoring resale potential.

### Use case #3 — Sponsored opportunities: someone else pays for our trial

- **Today.** When SCD ran a striker showcase in 2025, the entire ₹6L event cost — ground rent, kit, travel for 200 invitees, media — came out of our budget.
- **With OnlyKrida.** The same trial is co-branded with a sponsor under the Pattern A model from `docs/RESEARCH_BRAND_INTEGRATION.md`. Nivia or Decathlon pays ₹3–5L to be the "presenting partner of the SCD Striker Search." The opportunity post on OnlyKrida shows their logo. Applicants implicitly opt into one-time brand contact under DPDP-clean consent. We get the trial. Sponsor gets verified, filterable applicant data and brand association. Athletes get free kit at the event.
- **Surface.** `opportunities.sponsored_by_user_id` + `opportunities.sponsor_branding` schema additions; brand-flagged opportunity card render.
- **Outcome that justifies spend.** Entire trial cost externalised. SCD breaks even on its OnlyKrida subscription on **one** sponsored trial per year.

## 4. The "Sponsored Opportunities" pattern — from the team's chair

Why would I, the operations head, ever let Nivia or Decathlon attach to our trial?

**What's in it for us:**

1. **Cash.** Half my trial production budget gone. ₹3L from the brand on a ₹6L event is a rounding error to Nivia's marketing line; for me it's a quarter of my season's kit budget.
2. **Reach.** Brand pushes the opportunity to its own followers. Suddenly our trial appears on Nivia's IG to 400k people, half of whom we'd never have reached.
3. **Kit and gear for the event.** Brand throws in 200 boots and 300 jerseys. Athletes who couldn't afford studs now show up in studs.

**The risks I worry about:**

1. **Brand attaches to our colours and over-claims.** Suddenly Nivia is "official partner of SCD academy" when they only paid for one trial. **Mitigation:** contract template caps the association language at the per-opportunity level. The platform's `sponsor_branding` JSON only renders on that opportunity's card, never on our club page or roster.
2. **Brand contacts athletes after the trial.** A 14-year-old gets DM'd by a brand rep. POCSO and DPDP both blow up in our faces. **Mitigation:** the platform's `consent_to_sponsor_contact` flag (per Pattern A schema sketch) is opt-in per opportunity, never blanket; for under-18s, contact routes through guardian inbox only (per `concepts/regulatory-compliance.md` §3.3 rule 3).
3. **Owner sees brand co-marketing without sign-off.** **Mitigation:** every sponsored opportunity goes through a single approval surface in our admin panel before the brand logo appears.

Net: I'd take this in a heartbeat for one out of two trial windows per year. Two sponsored trials = ₹6–10L in offset cost = pays for OnlyKrida six times over.

## 5. Objections + responses

**"We have agents and freelance scouts who do this for free — why pay?"**
Agents work for the player, not us. They surface their own roster, not the wider universe. And they cost more than you think — three of our six freelance scouts cost ₹2L+ each per year, and they cover one district apiece. OnlyKrida covers every district, every Sunday, in software. The agents stay; OnlyKrida is the layer underneath them so they have richer data when they call us.

**"Indian football fans are toxic — what if our roster page gets spammed?"**
We don't run a roster page on OnlyKrida. The club has zero exposed comment surface. Athletes have profiles; we have an internal scouting workspace. Public-facing comments and DMs are rate-limited and moderated per IT Rules 2021 (`concepts/regulatory-compliance.md` §4). For under-18 athletes, adult-to-minor DMs are structurally blocked — scouts/coaches can only send templated interest messages routed to the parent inbox.

**"Our scouting network IS our moat — sharing it weakens us."**
We're not asking you to share it. Your scout queries are private. Your shortlists are private. Your trial-application pool is yours alone. OnlyKrida is the _supply layer_; your taste, your ranking, your relationships are still yours. What weakens the moat is being the only one not on it — Bengaluru FC and Mumbai City both have access to the same supply, and they'll out-fish you with the same data plus their existing scouts.

**"What about DPDP Act?"**
This is the right question and it's the one most operations heads forget to ask. OnlyKrida is built to the DPDP Rules notified 13 Nov 2025 with full obligations live 13 May 2027 (`concepts/regulatory-compliance.md` §2.1). Specifically: granular purpose-bound consent at the opportunity level, verifiable parental consent for under-18 via DigiLocker / e-KYC (DPDP §9 + Rule 10), consent ledger persisted, breach notification runbook, and our own designated grievance officer. **We do not become the data fiduciary for athletes you scout — they remain OnlyKrida's; we are a processor for the application data they explicitly share with us.** That distinction is what makes the legal team approve.

**"We've tried sports-tech, it failed."**
Most likely you tried Hudl, which is a video tool for coaching, not a scouting marketplace. Or you tried a one-off "talent app" that died because it had athletes but no scouts, or scouts but no athletes. OnlyKrida is two-sided by design: athletes are free forever, scouts pay, teams pay. The marketplace is already alive in Hyderabad and Bengaluru by the time we sign the SCD pilot. You ride a moving train, you don't build a station from zero.

**"We don't have time for another platform."**
Then don't use it. The pilot is structured so that our existing trial workflow runs unchanged — Google Form on top, OnlyKrida ingests the form responses through a webhook, augments them with verification tier and zone, returns a ranked list to our existing dashboard. Our team's net new tool burden in week one is zero. Adoption builds over three months, not three days.

## 6. Pricing & deal psychology

Indian B2B sports money is slow, but when it lands it lands big. The shape of the deal that works for me:

**Pricing floor.** Below ₹50,000/year I don't trust the platform to be serious. Free pilots that cost zero feel like marketing experiments and the budget owner ignores them. **Anchor at ₹1.2L–₹3L/year for a serious club tier** — that maps to OnlyKrida's "Club Pro" / "Academy Elite" lines from `docs/gbrain-seed/concepts/india-gtm-playbook.md` §8. ₹3L/year is roughly 5% of my scouting budget — it has to come out of "freelance scout #6" or "Hudl renewal," not out of new spend.

**Pricing ceiling.** ₹15L/year for federation-grade access is the upper end. Beyond that, sale moves from Director of Football to MD/owner and now I'm chasing a 9-month sales cycle. Stay below the line where I have signing authority.

**Per-trial vs subscription vs per-signing.**

- **Per-trial** is what brands buy, not what I buy. Too transactional.
- **Per-signing** is what agents earn, not what platforms earn. I will never agree to a percentage on the value of a player I sign — it conflates platform fees with agent commissions and creates conflict of interest.
- **Subscription is the only model I'll actually sign.** Predictable, fits my line item, renews on calendar.

Within subscription: **annual billed quarterly** is sweet spot. Monthly feels like SaaS, annual lump-sum scares procurement.

**Pilot vs annual contract.** I will not sign annual on first conversation. **3-month pilot at 90% off (so ₹7,500 against a ₹3L sticker) with a clean kill clause is the only way in.** If the pilot delivers one signing or one good shortlist, the annual contract is automatic. The first paying contract Anirudh lands in Hyderabad will look exactly like this — see Section 8 of `india-gtm-playbook.md`.

**Discount logic for early pilots.** Tier 1 partners — Sreenidi Deccan, SCD remnant academy, Telangana FA — get 90% off year 1 in exchange for: (a) case-study rights, (b) named-customer logo, (c) a public quote from the academy director on launch day. This is a zero-cash-cost lever that gives OnlyKrida the credibility receipts it needs for Tier 2 outreach.

## 8. The "Hyderabad goalkeeper makes Mumbai City U-17" moment

Sooner or later it'll happen: a kid applies to SCD, we reject him, he ends up signing for Mumbai City off OnlyKrida six months later. Is that a feature or a bug?

**Bug for ego, feature for brand.** The 19th-century answer is "we lost a player." The 21st-century answer is "we ranked him fairly, he hit Rising zone, the system worked, and the talent stayed in the ecosystem we helped build." I'd rather be the academy that _transparently let him leave_ than the academy that _secretly gatekept him_.

If we trained him for any window — even four weeks in a feeder camp — the platform's `coach_verified` history shows our hand on his development arc. When Mumbai City signs him, the public record reads: "developed at SCD feeder, scouted via OnlyKrida, signed at MCFC." That is brand-building, not brand-loss. And if the deal is structured as a transfer, we can negotiate a development fee at AIFF rates. ([AIFF transfer regs reference — verify]).

If we never touched him — pure rejection at trial — and he goes on to make MCFC, the public record is more nuanced: "applied to SCD, ranked Rising, signed by MCFC after appearing on OnlyKrida." That still puts SCD in the talent-spotting story. It's a public proof that we participate in a fair ecosystem.

**The PR play we can prep for in advance.** When the first OnlyKrida-discovered player makes any ISL squad — ours or not — Anirudh and the relevant academy operations head co-write a press piece for _Khel Now_ and _The Bridge_ (per `india-gtm-playbook.md` §7). The narrative is "Indian football just got its first scouted-online-to-signed pipeline." We are quoted regardless of whether the kid signed with us or not. We get credit for being on the platform at all.
