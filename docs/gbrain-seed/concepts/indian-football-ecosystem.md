Understanding the structural context OnlyKrida operates in. Essential for
product decisions, partnership strategy, and go-to-market.

**The pyramid (top to bottom):**
ISL (Indian Super League) → I-League → State leagues → District leagues →
School/college football → Grassroots / unorganized

**Key bodies:**

- AIFF (All India Football Federation): national governing body. Often slow-moving.
  Registration of clubs and players happens here — data opportunity.
- SAI (Sports Authority of India): government sports body. Runs training centers.
  Khelo India scheme falls under SAI + Ministry.
- State Football Associations: 36 state associations. Varying quality. Some have
  good records, many are paper-based.
- District Football Associations: where most grassroots talent lives. Almost no
  digital infrastructure.

**The discovery problem in numbers:**

- ~1.3 million registered football players in India (AIFF estimate, likely undercount)
- Most professional scouts cover only Goa, Maharashtra, West Bengal, Kerala
  (historically strongest football states)
- Northeast India (Manipur, Mizoram) has disproportionate football talent but
  almost no scout presence
- A talented 16-year-old in Telangana has almost no formal pathway to get seen
  unless he has connections or is in a major city

**Why WhatsApp groups dominate:**
Scouts trust their network. A recommendation from a trusted coach travels faster
through WhatsApp than any formal channel. OnlyKrida must earn a place in the
trust chain — verified data > informal recommendation over time.

**The ISL academies — potential partners:**
FC Goa, Mumbai City, Hyderabad FC, ATK Mohun Bagan all run academies.
They actively scout. They have budget. They need a better discovery tool.
Hyderabad FC academy is geographically relevant for Anirudh's network.

**Regulatory tailwinds:**
Khelo India Mission is pushing digitization of sports at state level. Government
data may become accessible via APIs. Aadhaar-linked athlete identity is being
piloted. OnlyKrida can position as the private infrastructure layer.
