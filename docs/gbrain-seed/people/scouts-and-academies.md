# Indian sports academies, federations, and scouts — partnership map

A working dossier of the institutions and individuals who run grassroots-to-pro pipelines in Indian sport, scoped to OnlyKrida's go-to-market: which academies adopt OnlyKrida to scout, and which scouts/coaches need to be the first 100 users on the platform.

**Scope**: India + Dubai/UAE (latter not yet mapped — placeholder).
**Sport priorities**: Football (ISL/I-League), Kabaddi, Cricket, multi-sport (athletics/badminton/hockey/basketball).
**Geography priority for warm intros**: Hyderabad first, then Bengaluru, then Goa/Kolkata/Mumbai.

A note on freshness: Indian football had two major structural shocks in the 2025-26 cycle — (a) Hyderabad FC's relocation to Delhi as **Sporting Club Delhi** in October 2025 ([SC Delhi Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SC_Delhi); [Business Standard, Oct 2025](https://www.business-standard.com/sports/football-news/sporting-club-delhi-launched-replaces-hyderabad-fc-as-capital-s-top-team-125101800604_1.html)) and (b) City Football Group divesting from Mumbai City FC on 26 December 2025 ([Mumbai City FC — Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Mumbai_City_FC)). Both reshape this map and create urgency.

## 1. ISL academies

The Indian Super League (ISL) currently has 13 clubs in the 2025-26 season ([2025-26 ISL Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025%E2%80%9326_Indian_Super_League)). All 142 academies that applied were rated by AIFF in Sept 2025; **only 4 hit four-star** (Alchemy, FC Madras, Minerva, Jamshedpur FC) and **only 3 ISL clubs hit two-star or above** in that cycle — FC Goa, East Bengal, Mohun Bagan SG, NorthEast United, Kerala Blasters were two-star ([Football Counter — AIFF Accredited Academies 2025-26](https://www.footballcounter.com/2025/aiff-accredited-academies-2025-26-full-updated-list/); [TechnoSports](https://technosports.co.in/aiff-name-2025-26-academy-accreditation-results/)). This rating gap is the wedge: weak academies need OnlyKrida more than strong ones.

### FC Goa

- **City**: Margao, Goa.
- **Academy structure**: Forca Goa Foundation runs grassroots; 1,500+ kids ages 6-14 in pipeline ([Forca Goa Foundation](https://www.forcagoa.org/)).
- **Named leadership**: Gavin Araujo (AFC A-license) is Head of Youth Development; Katz Naidoo (South African) is/was Technical Director of youth ([FC Goa Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FC_Goa); flag as 2022-2024 — re-verify).
- **Scouting infra**: No public scout-facing app; trial calendar published via fcgoa.in.
- **Pipeline outputs**: AIFF two-star accreditation 2025-26 ([Football Counter](https://www.footballcounter.com/2025/aiff-accredited-academies-2025-26-full-updated-list/)). Multiple alums in senior ISL squads.
- **Partnership angle**: FC Goa is the most credentialed ISL academy in the south. Goa is a 1-flight hop from Hyderabad. Foundation NGO structure makes it easier to pilot than corporate academies.

### Mumbai City FC

- **City**: Mumbai.
- **Academy structure**: U11/U13/U15/U19/U21 at Mumbai Football Arena ([Mumbai City FC Reserves Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mumbai_City_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)).
- **Named leadership**: (not public for 2026 — needs warm intro). Previous structure under City Football Group methodology.
- **Status flag**: City Football Group **divested** on 26 Dec 2025 ([Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Mumbai_City_FC)). Ownership/strategy is in flux — bad time for a cold pitch, good time for an opportunistic one ("we'll fill the CFG-tools gap").
- **Pipeline outputs**: U19 won MFA Super League 2024.
- **Partnership angle**: Wait 3 months for ownership to settle; then approach as "rebuild your scouting stack."

### Bengaluru FC

- **City**: Bengaluru (Anirudh's secondary geo).
- **Academy structure**: AIFF five-star rating 2024 — among India's best ([Football Counter, 2024](https://www.footballcounter.com/2024/bengaluru-fc-academy-awarded-5-star-rating-by-aiff/)).
- **Named leadership**: Renedy Singh — head coach, reserves & academy, appointed Aug 2025 ([Naushad Moosa Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naushad_Moosa)). Naushad Moosa, the longtime Head of Youth Development, **left in 2023** for NorthEast United and was named India U-23 head coach in May 2025 ([AIFF](https://www.the-aiff.com/article/naushad-moosa-appointed-india-u23-mens-national-team-head-coach)).
- **Scouting infra**: No public scout app; trials via bengalurufc.com.
- **Pipeline outputs**: Regular India national team feeders ([Bengaluru FC Reserves Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengaluru_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)).
- **Partnership angle**: Highest-quality academy in South India. Geographically next-door to Hyderabad. Padukone-Dravid CSE shares the city — bundle a Bengaluru pilot.

### Kerala Blasters FC

- **City**: Kochi.
- **Academy structure**: KBFC Young Blasters program + affiliated academies network across Kerala ([ISL press release](https://www.indiansuperleague.com/press-releases/kerala-blasters-fc-launches-kbfc-young-blasters-affiliated-academies-across-kerala)).
- **Named leadership**: **Rajah Rizwan** — Academy Director; **Tomasz Tchorz** (Polish, UEFA A) was Technical Director until Oct 2025 when he left for Sporting Club Delhi ([Kerala Blasters Reserves Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerala_Blasters_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)). New TD slot likely open as of late 2025.
- **Pipeline outputs**: AIFF two-star 2025-26 ([Football Counter](https://www.footballcounter.com/2025/aiff-accredited-academies-2025-26-full-updated-list/)).
- **Partnership angle**: They have the largest _fan base_ in ISL — onboarding KBFC unlocks a "fan-driven scouting" narrative. Affiliated-academies model means OnlyKrida could be deployed across a network, not just one club.

### Mohun Bagan Super Giant (formerly ATK MB)

- **City**: Kolkata.
- **Academy structure**: Mohun Bagan SG Youth and Academy — entered 2025-26 season with restructured U13/U15/U17/U18 squads ([Mohun Bagan SG Youth Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohun_Bagan_SG_Youth_and_Academy); [2025-26 Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025%E2%80%9326_Mohun_Bagan_SG_Youth_and_Academy_season)).
- **Named leadership**: Sanjoy Sen historically Youth Development Secretary — flag as 2022-2024, re-verify.
- **Pipeline outputs**: AIFF two-star 2025-26.
- **Partnership angle**: Bengal football's emotional gravity. Pair with East Bengal in a single Kolkata trip.

### East Bengal FC

- **City**: Kolkata.
- **Academy structure**: East Bengal School of Excellence launched 11 July 2025 — landmark new infra ([East Bengal Reserves Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/East_Bengal_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)).
- **Named leadership**: **Thangboi Singto** — Head of Football, appointed 7 May 2025 ([Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/East_Bengal_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)). Note: Singto is the same coach who quit Hyderabad FC mid-season ([India Sports Hub](https://indiasportshub.com/from-hyderabad-fc-to-sporting-club-delhi-inside-the-isls-great-relocation-gamble/)) — Anirudh likely has degrees of separation here.
- **Partnership angle**: Brand-new academy = no entrenched scouting tooling yet. **High-leverage**. Singto-Hyderabad link is a possible warm-intro vector.

### Odisha FC

- **City**: Bhubaneswar.
- **Academy structure**: New state-of-the-art residential academy in Cuttack — 2 full pitches, gym, dorms; ages 8-18, U9/U11/U13/U15/U18 ([Odisha FC Reserves Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odisha_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)). Also runs Odisha FC Digital Grassroots Academy ([odishafc.com](https://www.odishafc.com/news/details/odisha-fc-digital-grassroots-academy-launched)) — relevant precedent: this club is _already digitizing_.
- **Named leadership**: **Suvam Das** — Grassroots Manager. Coaches Sandeep Alhan, Kirti Kashyap.
- **Partnership angle**: They've already shown appetite for digital tooling. The "Digital Grassroots Academy" framing is a near-perfect lead-in for OnlyKrida. **Highest-fit ISL club for cold outreach.**

### Chennaiyin FC

- **City**: Chennai.
- **Named leadership**: Syed Sabir Pasha was Technical Director of Youth as of 2018 ([Chennaiyin FC Reserves Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chennaiyin_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)) — flag as stale, re-verify.
- **Partnership angle**: Tamil Nadu state-only youth model (U13/U15) means tightly localized scouting — OnlyKrida's geo-filter UX is a fit.

### Jamshedpur FC + Tata Football Academy

- **City**: Jamshedpur.
- **Academy structure**: TFA — established 1987, the oldest serious football academy in India ([Tata Football Academy Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tata_Football_Academy)). U-15 trial registration is publicly run ([fcjamshedpur.com](https://www.fcjamshedpur.com/news/jamshedpur-fc-kick-starts-registration-for-youth-team-u15-trials-for-the-tata-football-academy-tfa)).
- **Named leadership**: **Mukul Choudhari** — CEO of Jamshedpur FC. Historic TFA directors Chuni Goswami, Arun Ghosh long retired. Current academy head not publicly named — needs warm intro.
- **Pipeline outputs**: AIFF **four-star** 2025-26 ([TechnoSports](https://technosports.co.in/aiff-name-2025-26-academy-accreditation-results/)). 150+ cadets have represented India ([Avenue Mail](https://avenuemail.in/at-35-years-jamshedpurs-tata-football-academy-boasts-of-150-cadets-representing-the-country/)).
- **Partnership angle**: Tata is a high-prestige logo — a TFA pilot would be the strongest case study possible. Hard to crack without a Tata Sports relationship.

### NorthEast United FC

- **City**: Guwahati.
- **Named leadership**: Khalid Jamil historically Asst Manager + Head of Academy ([NEUFC Reserves Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northeast_United_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)) — flag as stale. Naushad Moosa was at NEUFC 2023-2025 before India U-23 role.
- **Pipeline outputs**: AIFF two-star 2025-26.
- **Partnership angle**: Northeast India is the densest grassroots football region in the country. NEUFC is the natural gateway. Lower priority for Anirudh's first 90 days.

### Punjab FC

- **City**: New Delhi (training base) / Punjab brand.
- **Academy structure**: RG Punjab FC Academy, Sector 44 B Chandigarh ([rgpunjabfc.com](https://rgpunjabfc.com/academy/)) — Roundglass-owned.
- **Named leadership**: (not public — needs warm intro).
- **Partnership angle**: Roundglass also runs RoundGlass Punjab FC in I-League — bundle outreach.

### Sporting Club Delhi (formerly Hyderabad FC) — **CRITICAL**

- **City**: New Delhi (relocated Oct 2025).
- **Backstory**: BC Jindal Group (Jindal Football) acquired the Hyderabad FC ISL license in July 2025 after the club had defaulted on player salaries; rebranded as SC Delhi ([Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/sports/football-news/sporting-club-delhi-launched-replaces-hyderabad-fc-as-capital-s-top-team-125101800604_1.html); [India Sports Hub](https://indiasportshub.com/from-hyderabad-fc-to-sporting-club-delhi-inside-the-isls-great-relocation-gamble/)).
- **Academy plans**: Building from scratch — youth academy, women's team, football schools across Delhi-NCR; investing in performance analytics and sports science ([New Kerala](https://www.newkerala.com/news/a/sporting-club-delhi-emerges-as-delhis-football-powerhouse-889.htm)).
- **Named leadership**: **Tomasz Tchorz** — moved to head coaching role at SCD from Kerala Blasters in Oct 2025 ([Kerala Blasters Reserves Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerala_Blasters_FC_Reserves_and_Academy)).
- **Implication for Anirudh**: The Hyderabad FC academy infrastructure (Gachibowli area) is **orphaned** as of late 2025. Whoever runs it now is a high-leverage local target. Anirudh should investigate whether the Hyderabad-side youth program continues independently, was rolled into SATS, or got picked up by Sreenidi Deccan.
- **Partnership angle**: SCD is being rebuilt from zero. _Now_ is the window to be their default scouting platform.

### Inter Kashi (new ISL entrant 2025-26)

- **City**: Varanasi (UP).
- **Academy structure**: **Atlético de Bharat Residential Academy**, opened 25 March 2025 in partnership with Sant Atulanand Convent School and Dr. Vandana Singh — U13/U15/U17 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inter_Kashi_FC); [Khel Now match preview](https://khelnow.com/football/indian-football-isl-inter-kashi-vs-mohammedan-sc-preview-202604)).
- **Partnership angle**: Brand-new academy in a brand-new ISL club. Unburdened by legacy tools. Vandana Singh is a named, accessible counterpart.

### Mohammedan SC (new ISL entrant 2025-26)

- **City**: Kolkata.
- **Status flag**: Bottom of ISL table 2025-26 — relegation pressure ([Outlook India](https://www.outlookindia.com/sports/football/inter-kashi-vs-mohammedan-sc-indian-super-league-2025-26-matchday-8-report)).
- **Partnership angle**: Distressed clubs adopt cheap differentiators faster.

## 3. Cross-sport academies

### Reliance Foundation Young Champs (RFYC)

- **Sport**: Football residential academy.
- **Named leadership**: **Robert Roelofsen** (Dutch, UEFA A, 34-yr career incl. VfL Wolfsburg) — Head of Youth ([Khel Now interview, 2026](https://khelnow.com/football/indian-football-exclusive-reliance-foundation-young-champs-robert-roelofsen-interview-202601)).
